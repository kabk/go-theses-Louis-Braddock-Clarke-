Alchemy - The Ouroboros The above is an image from "An Enigmatic Book of the Netherworld", which was found on a shrine in the tomb of king Tutankhamen. It contains the oldest depiction of an Ouroboros (tail-devouring snake) in history. It is similar to the Phoenix, representing self-creation and renewal. In Alchemy, it symbolizes the circular nature of the Great Work and the concept of "All in One", infinity, or wholeness. Alchemy - Islamic The name Alchemy is derived from the Arabic word al-kimiya, which likely comes from Khem, the original name of Ancient Egypt, meaning The Black Land, after the color of the fertile soil around the Nile. Alchemy was the secret art of Ancient Egypt, and along with Astrology, is one of the two oldest sciences and considered a gift from God. Islamic Alchemists picked up knowledge of the art from Hellenistic Egyptian and Greek sources, when they conquered those areas. They further advanced both the Philosophical and Practical sides of Alchemy through the middle ages. This knowledge was then re-discovered by the West, during the crusades and renaissance.
